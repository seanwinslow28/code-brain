If you tried Wednesday’s five lines, you’ve spent the last few days pasting them at the end of every AI request. After a week of that, the novelty wears off. The lines work, but typing them out (or remembering to copy them) gets old fast.

There’s a way to put your standing preferences into ChatGPT once and have them apply to every conversation automatically. The setting has been there the whole time. Most people have never opened it.

It takes about ten minutes. The hardest part is figuring out what to put in — and we’ll handle that with a starter set you can paste right now.

*Today: how to set up ChatGPT’s custom instructions, what to put in them, and what to leave out.*

## What are custom instructions

Custom instructions are how you tell ChatGPT to behave the same way in every chat once, instead of retyping it every time.

ChatGPT’s Personalization page is where they live. Anything you put on that page runs at the start of every new chat, like a permanent intro that happens before you even type your message.

The page has two parts that work together. **About you** is a small set of fields for the basics: your name, your job, anything else you want ChatGPT to remember about your situation. The field literally labeled **Custom instructions** is a single text box for behavior, style, and tone preferences. This controls how you want responses to sound, words to avoid, formatting rules.

## How to find them

1\. Open ChatGPT.

2\. Click your name or initial in the bottom-left corner.

3\. Click **Settings**.

4\. Click **Personalization** in the menu

5\. You’ll see a **Custom instructions** box at the top, **About you** fields below it (Nickname, Occupation, More about you), and a **Memory** section near the bottom.

## What to put in (and what to leave out)

This is where most people go wrong. They open the page for the first time, get excited, and dump in everything about their lives: their job, their kids, their company’s mission, the project they’re working on this month.

The rule that fixes this: **only put in what’s still going to be true in six months for almost every chat you start.**

Four kinds of things survive that test, and they map to different fields:

1\. **Who you are and what you do** goes in **Occupation** and **More about you**.

2\. **Tone preferences** go in **Custom instructions**.

3\. **Standing rules** goes in **Custom instructions**. (Wednesday’s lines belong here)

4\. **Phrases or words you don’t want AI to use** also go in **Custom instructions**. (Example: “Avoid ‘leverage,’ ‘optimize,’ or ‘transform.’*)*

## What does not belong in any of these fields:

- Anything tied to a specific project (that goes in the chat itself)
- Anything tied to a tool you’re using just once
- Anything tied to a specific time period. Like a launch, a deadline, a season

## A starter set you can paste right now

**Nickname**: just your first name, or whatever you want ChatGPT to call you.

**Occupation**: one short phrase. (Example: *“Marketing coordinator at a small nonprofit.”* *“Home daycare manager.”* *“Parent and small business owner.”* “ *Freelance graphic designer.”* Whatever describes most of what you use ChatGPT for.)

**More about you**: one or two sentences about context that isn’t tone-related. (Example: *“I write mostly for clients in the wellness industry. I prefer plain English over corporate language and I’m not interested in buzzwords.”)*

**Custom instructions**:

Paste this into the box:

> *Use a direct, plain tone. Short paragraphs. Don’t use words like “leverage,” “optimize,” “innovative,” “transform,” or “seamless.” Use plain words people would say. No emojis, no exclamation marks, no warm-up phrases like “Great question!” or “Sure!” Just the answer. If I’ve said something that’s off, push back instead of agreeing. Tell me what could go wrong. If you’re not sure about something, say “I don’t know.” Don’t guess.*

Fill in the About you fields, paste the Custom instructions block, save. Open a fresh chat afterward. The settings only apply to new chats, not ones already open.

Ask ChatGPT how to learn a language without custom instructions. The answer reads about how you’d expect: confident, a little hollow.

## What we learned

ChatGPT’s Personalization page has two parts: a few **About you** fields where you tell it the basics about yourself, and a **Custom instructions** box for how you want responses to sound. Both run before every message you send. The rule for what goes in: only what’s still true in six months for almost every chat. Tone, standing rules, who you are. Not projects, not deadlines, not anything that changes fast.
