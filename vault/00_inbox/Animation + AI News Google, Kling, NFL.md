This week at the intersection of animation and AI, **Google** releases a new video model it claims is the next step towards AGI and **Kling** enters the increasingly crowded AI feature animation space. Plus… sports?

Let’s get into it!

## Tools

This week at the annual **Google I/O** event, the tech giant announced **Gemini Omni**, which is a step up from their previous video generation models, so much so that they claim it will bring the world ever closer to Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). The biggest step-change for Omni is the ability to easily edit videos similar to what Google’s **Nano Banana** did for AI image generation last year. While I continue to be skeptical that video generation models will have much of a place in “serious” animation production (my bet is agentic workflows and task-specific AI tools will disrupt production instead), clearly there is a growing economy for AI-animated “slop” and Gemini Omni seems particularly well suited to this. ([via Mashable](https://mashable.com/article/gemini-omni-flash-ai-video-generation-google-io-2026))

## Film

Case in point, **Copán la Leyenda**, an AI-animated feature film seemingly using similar video generation tools has been released in Honduras. The filmmakers claim AI technology is the only way a country with a very small animation industry and even smaller budgets can create home-grown feature animation. But the film is being hit hard by critical and audience reactions calling the end result *“emotionally lifeless”*. ([via Cartoon Brew](https://www.cartoonbrew.com/artificial-intelligence/honduras-copan-la-leyenda-ai-feature-261199.html))

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DXtiYe5KrPU)

That’s not stopping **Kling AI** from getting into the AI feature animation business. The Chinese video AI start-up announced a partnership with **Evolutionary Films** to produce the animated film **Minibots**, which was previously revealed in 2024 as a film directed by **Tony Bancroft** (**Mulan**). It’s unclear if Bancroft is still involved with the AI makeover. ([via Variety](https://variety.com/2026/film/markets-festivals/minibots-kling-ai-evolutionary-films-cannes-1236753520/))

## Television

At a recent “For Your Consideration” **Emmys** event, **Matt Stone** and **Trey Parker** spoke about nearly 30 years of making **South Park**. Through their side company **Deep Voodoo**, the pair have started incorporating AI tools into the making of the hit comedy, which famously has incredibly fast turnarounds. Speaking of the technology, Stone said:

> *“What’s happening with AI is that it’s going to bring those tools that we enjoy into more beautiful animation and ultimately live action. I think the tech community has come and said that \[they’re\] going to take jobs. People have to realize that all those guys are doing is raising money. That’s just a marketing pitch. They’re out there selling something. I think what’s going to happen is that more tools are going to be available to more filmmakers, and just as many people will be employed. It will change things, but I think there has been a panic because of the way it has been presented by the tech guys.”*

([via Animation Magazine](https://www.animationmagazine.net/2026/05/matt-stone-and-trey-parker-discuss-the-success-and-stress-of-delivering-south-parks-record-breaking-season-at-fyc-event/))

## VFX

Meanwhile, AI continues to make inroads into VFX this week with the release of **Canvas** from **Beeble**. The program is an overall compositing system that incorporates various generative AI tools such as AI rotoscope, relighting, and video-to-video transformation into one place. ([via Animation World Network](https://www.awn.com/news/beeble-launches-canvas-node-based-ai-compositing-system))

![](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9E06L6d1b0Q)

## Sports

Startup **Utopai Studios** is continuing its partnership with professional athletes after their AI-animated short featuring NBA star **James Harden** was released earlier this spring. The company is now working with basketball Hall of Famer **Carmelo Anthony** to connect Utopai with other pro athletes hungry for AI content. ([via Animation Magazine](https://www.animationmagazine.net/2026/05/utopai-launches-partnership-with-nba-great-carmelo-anthony-with-ai-powered-anime-property/))

Finally, the “schedule announcement video” from the **Arizona Cardinals** NFL team spectacularly backfired when it was ridiculed online for its heavy use of AI. The **Green Bay Packers** clapped back with *“Your AI slop bores us”* attached to a behind-the-scenes look at their handmade stop-motion announcement video by **Stoopid Buddy Stoodios** (**Robot Chicken**). ([via Sports Illustrated](https://www.si.com/nfl/cardinals/onsi/nfl-world-ruthlessly-arizona-cardinals-schedule-release-video))
