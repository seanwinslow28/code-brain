# Around The Block — Ambassador Program Plan

## What This Is

A global correspondent network that turns local crypto insiders into the eyes and ears of The Block's adoption tracker. Thirty to fifty ambassadors, each covering their home country or region, posting social-first content about what's actually happening on the ground — not from Reuters rewrites, but from people who live it.

The program is funded through a sponsorship model where a meaningful cut goes directly to the people creating the content. The result: a distributed newsroom that no centralized outlet can replicate, powering the Around The Block brand with authentic, local-first reporting.

---

## Why This Works

The global crypto adoption story is fundamentally local. When Nigeria's SEC licenses its first exchange, the best person to explain what that means isn't sitting in New York — they're in Lagos, watching the queues at the registration portal. When Brazil's VASP compliance deadline hits, the ambassador in São Paulo knows which exchanges are scrambling and which ones were ready months ago.

This network gives The Block something competitors can't easily copy: real-time ground truth from 30–50 markets simultaneously, delivered in native social formats that audiences already engage with.

## Program Structure

### Three Tiers

The ambassador program runs on a three-tier structure. Everyone enters at Tier 1. Advancement is based on output quality, consistency, engagement metrics, and editorial judgment — not follower count alone.

---

### Tier 1: Local Correspondents (20–30 people)

**Who they are:** Crypto-literate locals with an active social presence and genuine interest in their country's regulatory and adoption landscape. They don't need to be professional journalists — they need to be plugged in and able to communicate clearly.

**What they do:**

- Post 4–6 pieces of social content per month covering their local crypto scene
- Flag breaking developments (new legislation, exchange launches, enforcement actions, CBDC news) to the editorial Slack as they happen
- Submit a brief monthly "State of [Country]" update (3–5 bullet points) that feeds the adoption tracker database
- Engage with the Around The Block community by replying to comments and participating in discussions on their posts

**Content formats they produce:**

- Twitter/X posts and short threads (primary)
- Instagram/TikTok-ready short takes when appropriate
- Quote-tweet and commentary on local news with the Around The Block lens
- Photos or screenshots from local events, conferences, or exchange apps that show adoption in action

**Compensation:**

- $500/month base retainer
- Performance bonus pool: up to $300/month based on engagement metrics and editorial quality scores
- Total potential: $500–$800/month

**Requirements to maintain Tier 1 status:**

- Minimum 4 posts per month published
- Respond to editorial pings within 24 hours during local business hours
- Monthly update submitted on time
- No factual accuracy issues flagged in consecutive months

---

### Tier 2: Regional Correspondents (8–12 people)

**Who they are:** Proven Tier 1 ambassadors who've demonstrated consistent quality, or experienced crypto journalists/analysts recruited directly for key markets. Each regional correspondent covers a cluster of 3–5 countries and serves as the editorial point person for their zone.

**What they do:**

- Everything Tier 1 does, but at higher volume: 8–10 pieces of social content per month
- Produce one deep-dive thread or carousel per month (the "Country Deep Dive" format from the content strategy)
- Contribute to the quarterly Regional Scorecard content
- Mentor and quality-check Tier 1 correspondents in their region
- Join a monthly 30-minute video call with the editorial team (async replay available)
- Pitch story ideas and flag emerging trends across their region

**Content formats they produce:**

- Everything from Tier 1, plus:
- Long-form Twitter/X threads (8–15 posts)
- Carousel graphics with editorial team support
- Occasional guest appearances on The Block's social channels or podcasts
- "What I'm watching" weekly regional roundup post

**Compensation:**

- $1,200/month base retainer
- Performance bonus pool: up to $500/month
- Revenue share: 15% of sponsorship revenue attributed to content in their region
- Total potential: $1,200–$2,500/month depending on sponsorship activity

**Requirements to maintain Tier 2 status:**

- Minimum 8 posts per month
- One deep-dive piece per month
- Tier 1 correspondent mentorship responsibilities fulfilled
- Quarterly performance review with editorial team

---

### Tier 3: Senior Correspondents (3–5 people)

**Who they are:** The best of the best — ambassadors who've proven they can deliver editorial-quality analysis and have become trusted voices in the Around The Block community. These people are effectively regional bureau chiefs.

**What they do:**

- Everything Tier 2 does, plus:
- Produce 12+ pieces of social content per month, including analysis-grade posts
- Write or co-write the quarterly "State of the Map" flagship content
- Represent Around The Block at major conferences and events in their region
- Participate in editorial planning for the adoption tracker (input on stage classifications, framework updates)
- Help recruit and vet new Tier 1 candidates in their region
- Available for rapid-response coverage when major news breaks in their zone

**Compensation:**

- $2,500/month base retainer
- Performance bonus pool: up to $800/month
- Revenue share: 25% of sponsorship revenue attributed to content in their region
- Conference/event coverage stipend: up to $1,000/quarter for travel and expenses
- Total potential: $2,500–$5,000+/month depending on sponsorship activity and events

## The Sponsorship-Funded Model

The entire ambassador program is designed to run on sponsorship revenue, with a transparent split that keeps ambassadors motivated and sponsors happy.

### How Sponsorship Revenue Flows

```
[SPONSOR] → [Around The Block Program Budget]
                    ↓
        ┌───────────┼───────────┐
        ↓           ↓           ↓
   Ambassador    Production   Operations
    Payouts       Costs        & Tools
     (55%)        (25%)        (20%)
```

**55% to ambassador payouts:** Base retainers, performance bonuses, and revenue share. This is the headline number you tell ambassadors and sponsors — more than half of every sponsorship dollar goes to the people on the ground.

**25% to production costs:** Graphic design (the stage-change templates, scorecards, maps), video editing support, any freelance editorial support needed.

**20% to operations and tools:** Platform subscriptions (social scheduling, creator management, analytics), Slack workspace, Airtable/Supabase for the tracker database, and program management overhead.

### Sponsorship Tiers for Brands

| Tier | Monthly Rate | What Sponsors Get |
|---|---|---|
| **Tracker Sponsor** | $5,000–$8,000/mo | Logo on the weekly Global Adoption Map graphic, mentioned in all "State of the Map" content, logo in ambassador bios |
| **Regional Sponsor** | $3,000–$5,000/mo | Sponsor a specific region (e.g., "LATAM coverage powered by [Brand]"), logo on all regional scorecard content, featured in regional deep dives |
| **Stage-Change Sponsor** | $2,000–$4,000/mo | Logo on every stage-change graphic (the flagship viral format), mentioned in stage-change posts |
| **Event Sponsor** | $1,500–$3,000/event | Fund an ambassador's conference coverage, co-branded event recap content, live social coverage during event |

### Revenue Targets to Sustain the Program

Back-of-napkin math for a fully funded program:

- 25 Tier 1 ambassadors at $600/mo average = $15,000/mo
- 10 Tier 2 ambassadors at $1,500/mo average = $15,000/mo
- 4 Tier 3 ambassadors at $3,000/mo average = $12,000/mo
- **Total ambassador payouts: ~$42,000/mo**

If ambassador payouts are 55% of total budget:
- **Total monthly program budget needed: ~$76,000/mo**
- **Annual budget: ~$915,000**

To hit that with sponsorships:
- 2 Tracker Sponsors at $6,500/mo = $13,000
- 4 Regional Sponsors at $4,000/mo = $16,000
- 3 Stage-Change Sponsors at $3,000/mo = $9,000
- 4 Event Sponsors at $2,000/mo average = $8,000
- **Total sponsorship revenue: ~$46,000/mo**

Gap of ~$30,000/mo can be covered by:
- The Block's existing sponsorship pipeline (cross-selling Around The Block as an add-on)
- Affiliate revenue from exchanges and platforms featured in coverage
- Premium content or data access (selling the tracker database to institutional clients)
- Scaling sponsorship as the audience grows

### Phased Budget Approach

You don't need $76K/month on day one. Phase it:

**Phase 1 (Months 1–3): Pilot — $15,000–$20,000/mo**
- 10 Tier 1 ambassadors in top markets
- 2 Tier 2 regional leads
- 1 Tracker Sponsor, 1 Stage-Change Sponsor
- Prove the model, build the content library, demonstrate engagement

**Phase 2 (Months 4–8): Scale — $35,000–$45,000/mo**
- Expand to 25 Tier 1 + 6 Tier 2 + 2 Tier 3
- Add Regional Sponsors as coverage zones fill out
- Introduce Event Sponsorships as ambassadors start attending conferences

**Phase 3 (Months 9–12): Full Network — $60,000–$80,000/mo**
- Full 30–50 ambassador roster
- All sponsorship tiers active
- Revenue share model kicking in for Tier 2 and 3

## Content Workflow

### How a Post Goes from Idea to Published

**Breaking news scenario (same-day turnaround):**

1. Ambassador spots a development (e.g., their country's central bank announces CBDC pilot)
2. Ambassador posts an alert in the #breaking Slack channel with a link and 2-sentence summary
3. Ambassador drafts a social post and drops it in #review within 2 hours
4. An editor (or Tier 3 senior correspondent) reviews for accuracy and tone — reacts with ✅ or suggests edits
5. Ambassador publishes from their own account, tagging @AroundTheBlock
6. Around The Block's main account retweets/reposts with additional context
7. If this triggers a stage change, the editorial team generates the stage-change graphic and publishes from the main account, crediting the ambassador

**Planned content scenario (deep dives, scorecards):**

1. Content calendar assigns the topic 2 weeks in advance
2. Ambassador submits a draft outline to their Tier 2/3 mentor
3. Mentor provides feedback within 48 hours
4. Ambassador produces the final content (thread, carousel text, etc.)
5. Production team creates any supporting graphics
6. Ambassador publishes on scheduled date
7. Main account amplifies

### Content Standards

Every ambassador post should:

- Lead with the local angle (what's happening on the ground, not a rewrite of a press release)
- Reference the adoption stage framework when relevant ("This moves [country] closer to Stage 4")
- Include at least one specific data point, quote, or concrete detail
- Be written in the ambassador's authentic voice — not corporate speak
- Tag @AroundTheBlock for tracking and amplification
- Use the designated hashtag system (#AroundTheBlock #CryptoAdoption #[Country])

What to avoid:

- Price speculation or trading advice
- Shilling specific tokens or projects
- Unverified rumors presented as fact
- Copy-pasting from other outlets without attribution

## Recruitment & Onboarding

### Who You're Looking For

The ideal Tier 1 ambassador:

- Lives in or is deeply connected to the country they'd cover
- Has 1,000+ followers on Twitter/X (or equivalent platform relevant to their market)
- Posts about crypto, fintech, or local finance at least a few times per month already
- Can write clearly in English (doesn't need to be perfect — clarity over polish)
- Has at least a working understanding of regulation, policy, or financial infrastructure in their market
- Is responsive and reliable — this matters more than follower count

Where to find them:

- Crypto Twitter communities organized by country/region
- Local crypto meetup organizers and event speakers
- Contributors to local crypto media outlets or blogs
- Active members of country-specific crypto Telegram/Discord groups
- University blockchain club leaders
- Existing Block readers who engage with global coverage content

### Application Process

1. **Open application form** (Typeform or similar) asking for:
   - Country/region of focus
   - Social media handles and follower counts
   - 3 recent posts they've made about their local crypto scene (links)
   - A short paragraph (100–200 words): "What's the one thing happening in your country's crypto space right now that nobody outside is paying attention to?"
   - How many hours per week they can dedicate

2. **Editorial review** — the team evaluates based on:
   - Quality and insight of their existing posts
   - How compelling their "one thing" answer is
   - Geographic coverage needs (prioritize underrepresented markets)
   - Responsiveness during the application process itself

3. **Trial month** — accepted applicants do one month as a "trial correspondent" with reduced expectations (2 posts minimum) and a $250 stipend. At the end of the month, both sides decide if it's a fit.

4. **Full onboarding** — those who pass the trial month get:
   - The full Tier 1 retainer
   - Access to the ambassador Slack workspace
   - The Around The Block content playbook (templates, style guide, framework reference)
   - A 1:1 onboarding call with their regional mentor
   - Brand assets (profile badge, banner template, bio language)

### Onboarding Kit Contents

- **The Framework:** Full explanation of the 7-stage adoption model, with examples of what each stage looks like
- **Content Playbook:** Template structures for each content format (news post, thread, deep dive), tone and voice guidelines, hashtag system, do's and don'ts
- **Tools Access:** Slack workspace invite, content calendar (Notion or Airtable), graphic request process, analytics dashboard
- **Brand Assets:** "Around The Block Ambassador" profile badge, banner templates sized for Twitter/X and Instagram, approved logo usage, bio template
- **Legal:** Simple contributor agreement covering content rights (non-exclusive license to The Block), payment terms, code of conduct, termination terms

## Community Management

### The Slack Workspace

The ambassador Slack is the nerve center. Channel structure:

| Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|
| #breaking | Drop links and 2-sentence summaries of breaking news from your market — speed matters here |
| #review | Post drafted content for editorial review before publishing |
| #general | Community discussion, questions, cross-market banter |
| #tips-and-resources | Share useful sources, tools, and techniques for covering your market |
| #sponsorship-updates | Transparency on sponsorship deals, revenue, and how the split works |
| #wins | Celebrate great posts, high engagement, stage-change coverage, milestones |
| #[region] channels | APAC, LATAM, EMEA, North America — regional coordination and mentorship |

### Keeping People Engaged

The biggest risk with any ambassador program is attrition. People get excited, post hard for two months, then drift. Here's how to fight that:

**Monthly leaderboard:** Rank ambassadors by a composite score (post count × average engagement × editorial quality rating). Top 3 each month get a bonus ($200, $100, $50). Make it visible in #wins.

**Quarterly "Ambassador of the Quarter" award:** Bigger prize ($500 bonus + featured profile on The Block's main channels), voted on by peers and editorial team.

**Cross-market collaboration:** Pair ambassadors from different countries to co-create content. "Brazil vs. Nigeria: Who's moving faster toward Stage 5?" — both ambassadors contribute, both audiences engage.

**Exclusive access:** Ambassadors get early access to The Block's research, invitations to editorial calls, and first look at major reports. Make them feel like insiders, not freelancers.

**Annual summit:** If budget allows, bring the top ambassadors together once a year — either physically at a major crypto conference or via a dedicated virtual event. This builds bonds that keep people in the program.

**Clear advancement path:** Every Tier 1 ambassador should know exactly what it takes to reach Tier 2. Publish the criteria openly. When someone advances, celebrate it in #wins.

### Performance Reviews

**Monthly:** Automated metrics review (post count, engagement rates, response times) shared with each ambassador privately.

**Quarterly:** 15-minute 1:1 between the ambassador and their regional mentor or the program manager. Covers: what's working, what's not, any support needed, advancement discussion.

**Annual:** Comprehensive review including ambassador self-assessment, peer feedback, editorial team evaluation, and compensation discussion.

## Metrics & KPIs

### Program-Level Metrics

- **Total social reach per month** across all ambassador posts
- **Total engagement** (likes, retweets, replies, shares) across the network
- **Stage-change coverage speed:** Average time from event to first ambassador post
- **Geographic coverage:** Number of countries with active, posting ambassadors
- **Sponsorship revenue vs. target**
- **Ambassador retention rate** (month over month)
- **Content accuracy rate:** % of posts with zero factual corrections needed

### Ambassador-Level Metrics

- Posts published per month
- Average engagement per post
- Editorial quality score (1–5, assigned by mentor/editor)
- Response time on breaking news alerts
- Monthly update submission rate
- Community participation (Slack activity, peer collaboration)

### Sponsor-Facing Metrics (for renewals and upsells)

- Total impressions on sponsored content formats
- Engagement rate on sponsored vs. non-sponsored content
- Geographic distribution of audience reached
- Brand mention volume and sentiment
- Click-through rates on any sponsor links

## Risk Mitigation

**Accuracy risk:** Ambassadors posting incorrect information about regulations. Mitigated by the editorial review flow, quality scoring, and the rule that stage-change announcements only come from the main account after editorial verification.

**Conflict of interest:** Ambassadors who also work for exchanges, projects, or lobby groups. Mitigated by requiring disclosure of all crypto-industry affiliations in the application, and a clear policy that coverage must be independent regardless of affiliations. Undisclosed conflicts = immediate removal.

**Inconsistent quality:** Some ambassadors producing great work, others phoning it in. Mitigated by the trial month, monthly metrics, quarterly reviews, and the tier structure itself — low performers stay at Tier 1 or are gracefully exited.

**Burnout / attrition:** People leaving after a few months. Mitigated by the community elements (leaderboard, awards, summit), clear advancement path, and competitive compensation relative to comparable programs.

**Regulatory risk in ambassador's country:** In some markets, publicly reporting on crypto could attract regulatory attention to the ambassador personally. Mitigated by allowing pseudonymous participation where legally appropriate, and never requiring ambassadors to disclose information that could put them at legal risk in their jurisdiction.

## How This Connects to the Adoption Tracker Agent

The ambassador network and the automated news-scanning agent are two halves of the same system:

**The agent catches the news.** It's scanning APIs, RSS feeds, and social signals 24/7 to surface every regulatory development, CBDC announcement, and enforcement action worldwide.

**The ambassadors add the context.** They explain what the news actually means on the ground, provide local color, verify whether a bill that passed actually changes anything in practice, and surface stories that never make it into English-language news feeds.

**Together, they feed the tracker.** The agent flags potential stage changes based on keyword and classification matching. The ambassadors confirm or dispute those assessments based on local knowledge. The editorial team makes the final call.

This is the moat: anyone can build a news scraper, but nobody else has 40 people on the ground in 40 countries telling you what the headlines miss.
