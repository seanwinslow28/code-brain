### Serena’s Desktop Design Explorations

- Modular approach with visually distinct sections
	- Three-column layout with varied thumbnail sizes creating hierarchy
	- Dark mode for data/markets sections, light mode for news/research
	- Jump-to-section navigation for better content discovery
- Hero section options:
	- Toggle between “latest” and “trending” content
- Revenue integration requirements:
	- Press releases must stay above fold (contractual)
	- Sponsored posts need “sponsored” labels within cards
	- Research pieces needed above fold per Jeff’s requirements
	- OKX ad placement in right rail performing well

### Design Feedback & Refinements

- Strong preference for first hero option with modular sections
	- Horizontal “peaks” create ownable thumbnail style
	- Four-level visual hierarchy from large to small thumbnails
	- Tab system for markets section highly effective
- Technical specifications:
	- 1440px base width, expandable to 1800px+
	- Column-based system scales naturally across screen sizes
	- Maintains visual separation without seamless blending
- Missing elements to address:
	- Dedicated revenue space above fold
	- Video integration for Gareth’s daily show
	- Election hub promotional area

### Claudine’s Mobile-First Bento Layout

- Instagram Stories-style hero with timer and swipe navigation
- Bottom tab navigation (Home, Prices, Data, Learn)
	- Preserves branding vs current mobile header
	- More app-like experience
- Modular widget system:
	- Most read articles section
	- Swipeable latest news
	- Media hub with podcast/video tabs
	- Prices widget and Pro upsell module
	- Commission research and featured research modules
- Revenue considerations:
	- Multiple inline banner options vs sticky banner
	- 320x50 ads repeating every 2 modules
	- Trade-off between tab bar and sticky revenue banner

### Technical Integration Decisions

- Web design should lead app design direction
	- App currently rudimentary, will follow web standards
	- Create ownable brand elements on web first
- Dark/light mode strategy:
	- News content on light backgrounds
	- Data/numbers on dark backgrounds for visual pop
	- Media sections potentially dark for Netflix/Spotify feel
- Mobile navigation approach:
	- Tab bar preferred over current three-item header
	- Requires revenue team discussion on sticky banner trade-offs
	- Potential scroll-triggered or search-activated menu solutions

### Next Steps

- Continue parallel explorations for one more week
- Serena: Integrate Claudine’s modularity concepts into desktop
- Claudine: Develop desktop version incorporating Serena’s sectional approach
- Both: Address revenue requirements (press releases, sponsored content, research above fold)
- Next meeting: Coalesce toward single direction with specific decisions on hero, research sections, and revenue placement
