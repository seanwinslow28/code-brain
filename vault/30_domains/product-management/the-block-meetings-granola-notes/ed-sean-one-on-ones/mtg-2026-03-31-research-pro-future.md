## Enhanced Notes

### Pro Product Assessment & Future Vision

- At peak, Pro generated 50% of The Block’s revenue ($10M of $20M total)
- Market shifted away from research needs post-FTX fallout
- Increased competition from Delphi, Messari, Blockworks (all undercharging/taking losses)
	- Messari cut research team, now AI-centric
	- Blockworks focusing on data/podcasts over research
- Both CEO candidates want Pro as core revenue pillar
	- Reliable SaaS model vs current 80% ad-based revenue
	- Annual subscriptions with high renewal rates until major market events

### Data-Centric Redesign Strategy

- Homepage will mirror data dashboard with customization
	- Similar to X/Twitter onboarding - topic selection at signup
	- Dynamic content based on user preferences and reading history
	- Categories: ETFs, tokenization, prediction markets, stablecoins
- Current Pro users plateau maintained purely for data access
	- First action is always data downloads/requests
	- Institutional focus on embedding into existing processes
- Goal: become so embedded in workflows that switching becomes too risky
	- Example: Messari client acknowledged Block superiority but stayed due to team comfort

### Community Building & Engagement Features

- Connect module potential transformation into Discord-like chat
	- Dedicated channels: stablecoins, DeFi, ETFs
	- Auto-publish news to relevant channels
	- Pro members can comment, non-members view-only
- Migrate 20K telegram subscribers to on-site news feed
- Regulatory constraints for institutional users limit chat functionality
	- Fidelity, Bitwise may not be able to participate
- CEO candidates propose:
	- Regular X Spaces/calls with community
	- Institutional dinners and sporting events for relationship building

### Simon AI & Marketing Improvements

- Simon AI needs market testing - no usage data available
	- Consider making free to broader audience for demand validation
	- Better suited for retail vs institutional users
- Poor promotion of new features across platform
	- Data dashboard charts launched rapidly but not advertised
	- Need dedicated space for feature announcements
	- Marketing page insufficient for showcasing capabilities
