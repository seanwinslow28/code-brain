### Homepage Strategy — Key Themes

- Current homepage is a “catch-all” — 10+ sections, most content buried below the fold
	- Scroll depth drops dramatically after the latest news module (~1,574px height)
	- Heat maps show no clicks/taps on article thumbnails on desktop or mobile
- Proposal: lean into hottest/trending news as the homepage’s primary purpose
	- Surface most-read stories in a 24-hour window rather than pure chronological feed
	- Chronological feed compared to pre-algorithm Facebook — forces users to “catch up” rather than discover what matters
	- News as the “entree”; surface adjacent content (related tokens, research, media) contextually after a user engages with a story
- Most homepage sessions are direct (bookmarked or typed URL), but many homepage visits originate from article pages via the logo/icon click
	- Can’t assume homepage = savvy users; article readers land there too

### Design Explorations (Erupkus)

- Tab system: reflows homepage content by category (news, prices, media, etc.) within a fixed scroll height
	- Open question: should clicking “Prices” reflavor the homepage or just navigate to the Prices page?
	- Could cookie the selection to personalize on return visits
- Story/carousel mechanism: auto-advancing format inspired by Instagram Stories and The Starting Block
	- Matt (dropped early) noted he likes the carousel view
	- Feels informative rather than navigational — shows content “flavors” quickly
- Thumbnail obscuring: reduce image space, add more copy
	- Rationale: heat maps confirm users read titles, not thumbnails
- Bottom fixed nav: frees vertical space, makes site feel more app-like
- Sponsored content: experimenting with making it look less distinct from editorial
- Desktop and mobile designs need to stay interconnected — mobile-first is limiting because it forces prioritization before desktop strategy is set
