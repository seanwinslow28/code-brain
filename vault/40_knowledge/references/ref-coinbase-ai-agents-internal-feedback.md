Crypto exchange Coinbase is testing AI agents that appear in internal communication channels, enabling staff to consult them as virtual leaders and strategic planning tools.

Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong [said](https://x.com/brian_armstrong/status/2045564806371983479) on X that the company is developing AI agents that integrate into Slack and email and operate as human-like teammates.

According to a screenshot shared by Armstrong, the two AI agents — named "Fred" and "Balaji" — are modeled after former executives [Fred Ehrsam](https://www.theblock.co/profile/347459/fred-ehrsam) and [Balaji Srinivasan](https://www.theblock.co/profile/346835/balaji-srinivasan).

"Fred" is designed as a strategic executive agent, offering high-level feedback and assisting staff in refining documents, strategies, and concepts. "Balaji" is positioned as an "agent of chaos and creativity," helping employees with long-term projects.

"Soon, it will be easy for any employee to spin up a new agent for themselves or their team," said Armstrong. "I suspect we will have more agents than human employees at some point soon."

Ehrsam, a co-founder of Paradigm, previously served as Coinbase's president from 2012 to 2017. Srinivasan was a former chief technology officer at Coinbase, known for his contributions to promoting crypto technologies and involvement in initiatives such as Coinbase Earn.

## Next steps

Armstrong also noted that these tools will be developed beyond being just replicas of real individuals.

"I think these employee agents should have their own name actually. Not a 'digital twin' of someone else." Armstrong wrote in a different [post](https://x.com/brian_armstrong/status/2045609574095630652) on X. "So that is next step."

The Block has reached out to Coinbase for further information.

Coinbase has been actively expanding into AI. In February, the company [rolled out "Agentic Wallets,"](https://www.theblock.co/post/389524/coinbase-rolls-out-ai-tool-to-give-any-agent-a-wallet) enabling AI agents to hold funds independently, send payments, trade tokens, earn yield, and execute onchain transactions.

The agentic wallet aligns with Coinbase's [x402 protocol](https://www.theblock.co/learn/391983/what-is-coinbases-x402-protocol), which is being developed to enable autonomous crypto payments without human intervention.
