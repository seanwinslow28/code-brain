This research report evaluates the reliability of OpenRouter’s free-tier models in 2026 for supporting OpenAI-format tool calling, specifically identifying a viable replacement for a Qwen3-14B-on-Ollama setup when local hardware (a Mac Mini) is unavailable. The central thesis is that **Qwen3-Coder** serves as the optimal replacement for headless tool-using agents due to its high tool-call accuracy, lack of reported technical quirks, and a massive 1M token context window.

The report provides a ranked comparison of several free-tier models based on accuracy, quota, and stability. High-performing models include **Qwen3-Coder** (82% accuracy), **Qwen3-14B** (86% accuracy), **NVIDIA Nemotron 3 Super** (88% accuracy), and **Claude Opus** (87% accuracy), all of which are noted for having no major reported quirks. In contrast, models such as **gpt-oss-120b** exhibit known issues with JSON-mode failures and inconsistent schema parsing, while **DeepSeek R1-0528**, **Mistral Large**, and **Gemma 3** are flagged for inconsistent retry behavior and occasional schema parsing errors.

While the report identifies Qwen3-Coder as the preferred choice for agentic workflows, it notes that while **Qwen3-14B** is a viable alternative, it is slightly less optimized for tool calling in agent-based tasks compared to the Coder variant. The findings suggest that for users transitioning from local Ollama instances to OpenRouter, selecting a model based on its specific optimization for code generation and tool-calling stability is critical for maintaining the reliability of headless agents.
