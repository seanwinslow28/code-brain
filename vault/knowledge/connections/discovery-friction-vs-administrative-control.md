## Synthesis

The tension between decentralized discovery and centralized control reveals a fundamental trade-off in infrastructure design: flexibility versus consistency. In Sean's personal vault, the lack of a centralized catalog forces him to manually integrate disparate tools, increasing cognitive load and maintenance overhead. Conversely, in his professional or team contexts, centralized marketplaces reduce friction but introduce dependency on a single authority. This duality impacts how he scales his agent fleet, as personal automation requires robust manual curation while team automation benefits from enforced standardization.

## Implications

- Sean must build robust manual curation processes for his personal agent fleet to compensate for the lack of a unified plugin ecosystem.
- Scaling his personal infrastructure requires accepting higher maintenance costs in exchange for the flexibility of decentralized tools.
- He should leverage centralized mechanisms where possible in team settings to reduce onboarding friction and ensure consistency.
