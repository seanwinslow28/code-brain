## Synthesis

There is a fundamental tension between the 'Stammering Precision Pattern' in Kaufman's work—where subtext is encoded in the gap between banal surface dialogue and emotional thesis—and the 'Personal Agentic Intent Engineering' required for Sean's job hunt. Kaufman's characters fail to articulate their actual problem, manifesting it as an inability to perform mundane tasks. In contrast, Sean's agentic systems must explicitly articulate intent to avoid 'Token Waste' and 'Automation Failure'. The insight is that while creative depth requires hiding the thesis, operational reliability requires exposing it; Sean must toggle between these modes to prevent his creative work from becoming opaque to his own automation infrastructure.

## Threads

### [[Screenplay Craft Architect]]

> The pattern: Kaufman writes dialogue that is one conversation on the surface and a different conversation underneath, where the surface conversation is banal and the underneath conversation is the emotional thesis of the character.

### [[Personal Agentic Intent Engineering]]

> Intent Engineering is the practice of explicitly defining the goal state to prevent agents from drifting into irrelevant tasks.

### [[Token Waste]]

> Token Waste occurs when agents process context that does not directly contribute to the immediate operational goal.

## Implications

- Sean must design his creative prompts to explicitly state the 'emotional thesis' even when the scene's dialogue is banal, preventing his agents from misinterpreting the subtext as the primary instruction.
- The 'Stammering Precision' technique cannot be automated directly; it must be manually engineered into the scene structure because agents lack the nuance to generate 'banal surface' that reliably encodes 'emotional thesis' without explicit direction.
