## Synthesis

The 'Two Audiences' concept from marketing intersects with the need for robust Agent Health Monitoring. Marketing strategies must not only target humans but also ensure their messaging is legible to AI agents, which may rely on structured formats like daily notes. If Agent Health Monitoring systems fail — such as due to incomplete or inconsistent note data — AI agents may misinterpret marketing content, reducing its effectiveness for both human and algorithmic audiences.

## Threads

### [[Two Audiences]]

> Marketing now serves two audiences at once. It serves the humans it has always served, and the agents that increasingly read, summarize, compare, and recommend on behalf of those humans before any human decision gets made.

### [[Agent Health Monitoring]]

> The biggest shift in thinking — start asking AI models to do more for you, especially if you're a PM that isn't the most technical person.

## Implications

- Failure to maintain consistent, high-quality Daily Notes could result in Agent Health Monitoring systems failing to properly interpret marketing content, leading to poor AI-driven decisions.
- Marketing teams that neglect legibility for agents may face decreased visibility in algorithmic recommendations, even if their brand is strong and persuasive to humans.
