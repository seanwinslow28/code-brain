## Synthesis

The core tension lies in the conflict between the efficiency of private 'Deep Work' and the necessity of public 'performance' during an interview. When a candidate internalizes their reasoning to maintain flow, they become invisible to the evaluator, who can only score the visible 'judgment' of context loading and model challenging. This creates a structural requirement for the candidate to deliberately externalize their cognitive process, effectively turning their private workflow into a public artifact to satisfy the evaluation mechanism.

## Threads

### [[Vibe-Coding Interview Format]]

> A senior person figures out how to load context, challenge the model, and review what comes back, and the junior across the team never gets to watch how any of that judgment works.

### [[Deep Work]]

> Sean is preparing for 'vibe-coding' interviews where he must demonstrate his AI fluency. He needs to bridge the gap between his private 'Deep Work' and the public 'performance' required by the interview format, ensuring his 'narrate before you prompt' habit is robust enough for scrutiny.

### [[Two Audiences]]

> The tension arises because the candidate must perform their cognitive load in a way that is legible to an observer without breaking the flow of the work.

## Implications

- Sean must treat his interview preparation as a performance engineering problem, not just a technical one, requiring him to rehearse the 'narrate before you prompt' habit under simulated public-witnessing pressure.
- The evaluation metric shifts from 'correctness of output' to 'clarity of process,' meaning Sean's resume and portfolio must highlight his ability to externalize reasoning, not just his final code artifacts.
