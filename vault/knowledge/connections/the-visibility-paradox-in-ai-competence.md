## Synthesis

There is a fundamental tension between the efficiency of private AI work and the pedagogical value of public AI work. When Sean optimizes his job-hunt artifacts for pure efficiency (private tabs, hidden prompts), he creates an 'Accountability Gap' where his competence is invisible to evaluators. Conversely, when he forces visibility (public recording, narrated prompts), he risks breaking the flow of 'Deep Work' but gains the necessary signal for 'Vibe-Coding' interviews. The consequence is that Sean must treat his job-hunt artifacts not as final products, but as 'teachable posts' that expose the judgment layer, effectively turning his private AI sessions into public evidence of his 'Agentic Engineering' skills.

## Threads

### [[Two Audiences]]

> Private AI work helps the person at the keyboard. Public AI work helps the company learn.

### [[Vibe-Coding Interview Format]]

> A senior person figures out how to load context, challenge the model, and review what comes back, and the junior across the team never gets to watch how any of that judgment works.

### [[Deep Work]]

> A good prompt disappears into one person’s chat history. A clever correction stays inside one employee’s browser tab.

## Implications

- Sean must deliberately 'stage' his AI interactions during portfolio reviews to expose the 'judgment' layer, even if it feels artificial, to satisfy the 'Two Audiences' requirement of hiring managers.
- The 'narrate before you prompt' habit must be maintained during live interviews not just for clarity, but as a defensive mechanism against the 'vanishing' of his best work into private tabs.
- Sean’s portfolio projects should be structured as 'teachable posts' rather than just functional code, explicitly highlighting the 'context loading' and 'model challenging' steps that are usually invisible.
