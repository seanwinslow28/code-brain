## Synthesis

The tension lies between the need for precise control (abstraction layer) and the desire for broad problem-solving (intent engineering). When users go 'one abstraction layer up,' they gain flexibility but risk losing precision; however, by combining this with 'intent engineering'—asking AI to solve 'annoying' problems—they unlock capabilities that were previously invisible. The consequence is a shift from tool-user to system-architect, where the human defines the problem space and the AI navigates the solution space, enabling Sean to scale his personal productivity beyond linear effort.
