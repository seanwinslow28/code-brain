## Synthesis

There is a fundamental tension between the speed of agent execution and the slowness of human trust formation. Agents can complete tasks in seconds, but users only grant autonomy when they perceive a lack of correction. This gap creates a risk where high-speed agents appear healthy on standard dashboards while silently eroding user trust through repeated corrections. The consequence is that product teams must measure 'correction rate' as a leading indicator of autonomy, rather than relying on completion metrics which mask the underlying friction.
