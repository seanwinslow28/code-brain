## Synthesis

The tension between rigid structural constraints and emergent creative output appears in both Sean's screenplay craft system and his agentic infrastructure. In screenwriting, 'modular engineering' forces characters into mutually exclusive reference pools to generate conflict, mirroring how agentic systems rely on strict interface definitions to prevent state corruption. This pattern reveals that creativity in short-form media, like reliability in agent fleets, depends on the deliberate engineering of failure modes and communication gaps rather than seamless harmony. The consequence is a unified design philosophy where 'diagonal communication' in dialogue parallels the 'accountability gaps' or 'provider fallbacks' in technical systems—both are features, not bugs, that drive the system forward.
