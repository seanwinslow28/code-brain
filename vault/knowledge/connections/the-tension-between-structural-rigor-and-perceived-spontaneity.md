## Synthesis

There is a fundamental tension between the need for rigorous structural engineering in creative production and the audience's desire for perceived spontaneity and emotional authenticity. This pattern manifests when creators use highly constrained mechanical processes—such as stripping scripts to 'bare bones' or writing dialogue by hand to force natural cadence—to generate outputs that feel unscripted and organic. The consequence is that the most resonant creative work often emerges from the strictest adherence to mechanical constraints, rather than from free-form exploration. This insight challenges the assumption that creativity requires freedom from structure, suggesting instead that structure is the engine of resonance.
