## Synthesis

Vibe-coding interviews invert the traditional pair-programming dynamic by forcing the human to act as the navigator while the AI assumes the driver role, creating a tension where the human must constantly intervene to correct the model's drift. This inversion requires the candidate to manage a distributed agency where the 'driver' shifts between the human, the model, and the test suite based on real-time verification. The consequence is that seniority is no longer defined by the ability to write code, but by the ability to diagnose when the current agent (human or AI) is failing to maintain the plan's integrity.

## Implications

- Sean must design his portfolio artifacts to explicitly show the moments where he intervened to correct the AI's plan, rather than just the final output.
- Hiring managers will need to evaluate the 'repair' moves in the interview, not just the correctness of the code generated.
- The definition of 'senior' in an agentic workflow shifts from code volume to the frequency and quality of role-switching decisions.
