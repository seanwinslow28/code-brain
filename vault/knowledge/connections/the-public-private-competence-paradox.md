## Synthesis

The tension between private efficiency and public pedagogical value creates a critical bottleneck in Sean's job hunt. When artifacts are optimized for private speed, they become opaque to the hiring manager, who cannot verify the depth of Sean's intent engineering. This forces a strategic choice: prioritize the immediate utility of the code or the long-term signal of the thought process. The consequence is that 'vanishing' work into private tabs directly undermines the 'Agentic Engineering Signal' required to secure a role.

## Threads

### [[Two Audiences]]

> Private AI work helps the person at the keyboard. Public AI work helps the company learn.

### [[Agentic Engineering Signal]]

> An MCP makes it interactive — they can ask `find_contradictions()` and watch typed reasoning edges return as JSON. That's a 10x stronger signal than 'read this 90-second 4Q artifact.'

### [[Intent Engineering]]

> Intent engineering represents a shift from telling an AI agent *how* to do things to encoding *what it should want* — its objectives, success criteria.

## Implications

- Sean must refactor his portfolio projects to expose the 'intent' layer, not just the output, to satisfy the hiring manager's need for reverse-engineering competence.
- The 'Vibe-Coding Interview Format' must be adapted to include public-facing artifacts that demonstrate the 'narrate before you prompt' habit.
