## Definition

A critical, system-level constraint on the fidelity of research outputs generated by LDR and other agents. Citation quality collapses when topics are too complex or multi-target, leading to fabricated entities, owners, and URLs. This phenomenon is exacerbated by the lack of LDR upgrades, which limits its ability to handle advanced tasks.

## Context

Citation Quality is crucial for Sean’s knowledge vault because it ensures that information synthesized from external sources is not only accurate but also verifiable. Poor citation quality undermines the integrity of generated notes and makes them unreliable for decision-making.

## Evidence

> Citation quality collapse — even when LDR completes, Qwen3-14B can't ground citations across multiple targets and confidently writes fabricated entities, owners, and URLs.

> The system prioritizes quality, efficiency, and scalability in research processing.
