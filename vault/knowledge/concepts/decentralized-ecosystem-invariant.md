## Definition

A structural pattern where the absence of a centralized authority or unified catalog forces the system to rely on a fragmented network of independent repositories and community-driven standards. This fragmentation creates a high-friction discovery layer, requiring users to manually aggregate disparate sources rather than consuming a curated, unified interface. The system's integrity depends on the user's ability to navigate and synthesize these isolated nodes without a single source of truth.

## Context

Sean is building a complex agent fleet and knowledge infrastructure. Understanding that the underlying hardware and software layers lack a unified catalog is critical for managing expectations about integration complexity and the manual effort required to maintain system coherence.
