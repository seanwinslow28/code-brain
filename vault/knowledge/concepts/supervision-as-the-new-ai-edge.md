## Definition

Supervision is the cognitive discipline of evaluating machine-generated outputs against a specific recipient's needs and contextual constraints, rather than focusing on the generation process itself. This skill replaces speed and polish as the primary differentiator because AI tools have commoditized the ability to produce structurally sound, aesthetically clean drafts rapidly. The value lies in the judgment required to distinguish between 'usable-looking' and 'actually usable,' requiring the human to act as a senior editor who understands the gap between surface-level correctness and substantive utility. It shifts the locus of value from the prompt engineer to the critical reader who can identify what needs to stay, what must go, and what requires verification.

## Context

Sean is navigating a job hunt and product management landscape where technical execution is increasingly automated. Understanding that supervision is the new edge allows him to position his value not in how fast he can generate content, but in his ability to curate, verify, and refine AI outputs for high-stakes professional contexts like job applications or product strategy.

## Evidence

> Now everyone got fast at the same time, which means speed is still useful but it isn’t rare anymore. Polish is cheap now too — structure, clean formatting, executive-summary language, the whole package of things that used to make a document look as if somebody had really sat with it.

> The valuable person is the one who can tell the difference between usable-looking and actually usable. That is a different skill from the one most people were practicing last year. It is less about typing the perfect prompt and more about reading the answer with enough context to know what should stay, what should go, what needs checking, and what would make the work more useful to the person receiving it.

## Examples

- Using a senior-editor-supervisor prompt to inspect any AI draft before sending it to ensure it represents the user's actual intent and quality standards.
- Focusing on the judgment that happens after the model gives you something polished, rather than the prompt that generated it.
