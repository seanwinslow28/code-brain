## Definition

The comprehension-audit operates as a feedback loop between AI-generated content and its source material, ensuring that the output remains faithful to the original inputs. It introduces a dependency between content creation and verification, where any deviation from source fidelity must be flagged through iterative self-evaluation. This mechanism enforces a cost of accuracy — increased time and caution in the generation process — which influences how automation is balanced against manual oversight.

## Context

For Sean, the comprehension-audit ensures that AI-generated notes and summaries are accurate reflections of their sources. This is particularly important in his Daily Note Generation process, where trust in the content relies on this verification step. It also ties into his broader efforts to maintain quality across vault synthesizers and agentic workflows.
