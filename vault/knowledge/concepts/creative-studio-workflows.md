## Definition

A producer/consumer pattern where creative outputs are generated by agents but depend on live data inputs from external systems like Slack or Calendar. When these dependencies fail, the workflow halts and requires manual intervention, exposing a gap between automation's potential and infrastructure constraints. This reveals an underlying mechanism where the autonomy of creative tasks is contingent on real-time connectivity, which is often not guaranteed in current setups.

## Context

For Sean, this matters because it directly impacts his ability to automate creative workflows. Without real-time data from external systems, even the most sophisticated agents cannot perform as intended, which increases manual overhead and disrupts his creative process.

## Evidence

> Alienware and ComfyUI are OFFLINE, blocking the full creative-studio workflow mesh.

> The inability of autonomous agents to connect with live MCP servers creates a blocking tension between Infrastructure Status and Creative Studio Workflow Execution.

## Examples

- Alienware and ComfyUI are OFFLINE
- agents cannot hit MCP servers for interactive creative tasks
