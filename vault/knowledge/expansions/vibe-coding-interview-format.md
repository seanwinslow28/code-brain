## What this is

Critiques from two external reasoners (gpt-5.5 via Codex CLI, Gemini 3 via Anti-Gravity CLI) of [[vibe-coding-interview-format]]. The synthesizer describes what the concept is; this expansion proposes what's missing.

## From Codex (gpt-5.5)

1. **Add “interaction analysis,” not just interview performance.**  
   Anchor it on Lucy Suchman’s *Plans and Situated Actions: The Problem of Human-Machine Communication*. The missing move is: treat the interview as a situated human-machine-human interaction, not a test of “AI fluency.”

   Sentence pattern to add: “The candidate is not merely solving the task; they are repairing, redirecting, and re-framing a live plan in front of another human.”

   This unlocks a stronger Substack essay or interview runbook: **“How to Make Agentic Judgment Legible.”** Right now the concept risks sounding like “talk while you code.” Suchman gives Sean language for the deeper claim: competence appears in how someone handles breakdowns between plan, tool, context, and observed reality.

2. **Add “pair-programming driver/navigator inversion.”**  
   Anchor it on Laurie Williams and Robert Kessler’s *Pair Programming Illuminated*. Vibe-coding interviews are not just solo coding with AI; they invert pair programming. The AI is sometimes driver, sometimes junior pair, sometimes compiler, sometimes unreliable navigator. The human candidate’s job is role assignment.

   Sentence pattern to add: “In a vibe-coding interview, the key skill is not prompting; it is deciding who is driving now: human, model, test suite, interviewer, or prior spec.”

   This unlocks an **agentic interview scorecard** Sean can ship: categories like context loading, delegation, intervention timing, verification, and role-switching. The current concept can describe the performance burden, but it cannot yet produce a hiring rubric that distinguishes a senior agentic engineer from someone who just prompts fluently.

3. **Add “cognitive apprenticeship / making tacit judgment visible.”**  
   Anchor it on Allan Collins, John Seely Brown, and Susan Newman’s essay “Cognitive Apprenticeship: Teaching the Crafts of Reading, Writing, and Mathematics.” The missing facet is that vibe-coding interviews are apprenticeship theater: modeling, coaching, scaffolding, articulation, reflection, and exploration compressed into an onsite.

   Sentence pattern to add: “The interview rewards candidates who can articulate tacit judgment without flattening it into a script.”

   This unlocks a **portfolio artifact** Sean does not currently have: a recorded “AI work sample” annotated like a teaching case. For example: timestamped context-loading choices, model challenges, rejected outputs, verification moves, and interviewer-facing narration. The current concept says “narrate before you prompt”; cognitive apprenticeship turns that into a repeatable artifact: **show the invisible craft without pretending the craft is linear.**
