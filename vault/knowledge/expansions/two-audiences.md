## What this is

Critiques from two external reasoners (gpt-5.5 via Codex CLI, Gemini 3 via Anti-Gravity CLI) of [[two-audiences]]. The synthesizer describes what the concept is; this expansion proposes what's missing.

## From Codex (gpt-5.5)

1. **Add “Exegesis Mode” for making hidden judgment legible**
   - **What to add:** A mode where the artifact includes a short interpretive layer explaining *why this move was made*, not just what was built. Sentence pattern: “I chose X over Y because the real constraint was Z.”
   - **Anchor:** Donald Schön, *The Reflective Practitioner* — especially “reflection-in-action.” Schön’s key move is that expert work is not just execution; it is a conversation with the materials of the situation.
   - **Unlocks:** Sean can turn Code-Brain outputs into **portfolio case notes** and **agent decision records** that prove taste under uncertainty. Current concept says “make work public”; Schön adds the missing method for exposing situated judgment without bloating the artifact into a tutorial.

2. **Add “Worked Example as Transmission Unit”**
   - **What to add:** Treat each public artifact as a replayable worked example: initial state, constraint, failed move, correction, final move, transferable rule. Sentence pattern: “The first prompt failed because __; the repair was __; the reusable rule is __.”
   - **Anchor:** John Sweller, “Cognitive Load During Problem Solving: Effects on Learning” and the worked-example effect in cognitive load theory.
   - **Unlocks:** This gives Sean a stronger format for **Substack essays**, **agent runbooks**, and **vibe-coding interview artifacts**. “Two Audiences” currently distinguishes user vs observer, but it does not specify the pedagogical payload. Worked examples turn private agent traces into teachable competence without forcing readers to infer the lesson from raw logs.

3. **Add the contradicting frame: “Competitive Advantage Requires Some Work to Stay Private”**
   - **What to add:** A secrecy/publicness boundary: not all high-value AI work should become observable. Public artifacts should expose judgment patterns, not necessarily the whole operating system. Sentence pattern: “Show the decision grammar; hide the proprietary loop.”
   - **Anchor:** Michael Polanyi, *The Tacit Dimension* — “we know more than we can tell” is the canonical counterweight here. Pair with Eugene Wei’s essay “Status as a Service” for why public artifacts also become status games, not just learning infrastructure.
   - **Unlocks:** This lets Sean produce a **portfolio disclosure policy**, **agent-fleet demo boundary**, or **public/private knowledge runbook**. The current concept risks overcorrecting toward visibility. This missing facet helps him decide what to publish, what to redact, and what to package as proof without giving away the compound advantage of Code-Brain itself.
