## What this is

Critiques from two external reasoners (gpt-5.5 via Codex CLI, Gemini 3 via Anti-Gravity CLI) of [[unified-roadmap]]. The synthesizer describes what the concept is; this expansion proposes what's missing.

## From Codex (gpt-5.5)

1. **Add “Assumption Ledger” mode**
   - **What to add:** Treat the roadmap as a ledger of explicit bets, not just a synthesis artifact. Each phase/task should carry: assumption, evidence source, confidence, expiry date, falsifier, and next test.
   - **Anchor:** Annie Duke, *Thinking in Bets*; also Jeff Bezos’s “one-way door / two-way door” decision framing from the 2015 Amazon shareholder letter.
   - **Unlocks:** A job-hunt decision artifact Sean currently lacks: a **career strategy bet register**. Instead of “Karpathy and Nate modified the plan,” the roadmap can say: “I am betting that flagship artifact depth beats application volume until June 15; falsifier is <N recruiter screens/week.” This turns the roadmap into a decision instrument, not a chronology.

2. **Add “Wardley Map” mode**
   - **What to add:** A strategic landscape layer that maps job-hunt artifacts by user need, visibility, maturity, and differentiation. The current concept says “unified”; Wardley mapping would show where the roadmap is over-investing in commodities versus under-investing in differentiators.
   - **Anchor:** Simon Wardley, *Wardley Maps* / “On Being Lost” essays.
   - **Unlocks:** A **portfolio-positioning one-pager** for AI-PM / agentic-engineering roles. Example: “Resume polish” is commodity; “intent-engineering MCP demo” is custom/differentiated; “agent fleet observability dashboard” may be productizable proof. This helps decide which artifacts deserve July-level polish and which should stay merely adequate.

3. **Add “Narrative Spine” mode**
   - **What to add:** A constraint that every roadmap phase must ladder into a coherent public story: inciting problem, failed conventional approach, personal discovery, artifact proof, transferable lesson. Right now “unified roadmap” risks becoming internally useful but externally inert.
   - **Anchor:** Barbara Minto, *The Pyramid Principle*; also Andy Grove, *High Output Management*, especially the idea of managerial output as the output of the organization plus adjacent influence.
   - **Unlocks:** A **Substack-to-portfolio pipeline**. Each roadmap milestone becomes either an essay, demo page, runbook, or interview narrative. Sentence pattern: “I used to think ROADMAP was about coordinating tasks; the real problem was DECISION BOTTLENECK; I built ARTIFACT to make the bottleneck observable.” This prevents Sean from sounding like he built a private productivity maze instead of a transferable agentic-engineering operating system.
