## What this is

Critiques from two external reasoners (gpt-5.5 via Codex CLI, Gemini 3 via Anti-Gravity CLI) of [[research-prompt-autoresearch-agentic-polymarket-crypto-cash-flow]]. The synthesizer describes what the concept is; this expansion proposes what's missing.

## From Codex (gpt-5.5)

1. **Add “forecast ledger mode,” anchored on Tetlock & Gardner’s _Superforecasting_**

   Missing piece: the concept says “structured exploration,” but not **forecast discipline**. Add a section where every research prompt must emit: `claim -> base rate -> probability -> disconfirming evidence -> update trigger -> review date`.

   Exemplifies it: Philip E. Tetlock and Dan Gardner, _Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction_.

   Unlocks: an executable **Polymarket research runbook** instead of a vague topic note. Sean could ship an agent spec that turns autoresearch into calibrated forecast updates, with Brier-score-style self-auditing. Current failure mode: “agent summarizes market narratives.” New artifact: “agent maintains a falsifiable thesis book.”

2. **Add “vote values, bet beliefs,” anchored on Robin Hanson’s futarchy essay**

   Missing piece: Polymarket is treated as a cash-flow/research surface, but not as a governance primitive. Add the contradiction: markets are good at aggregating beliefs, bad at choosing values. Sentence pattern: `Use markets to price whether X will happen; do not let markets decide whether X should matter`.

   Exemplifies it: Robin Hanson, “Shall We Vote on Values, But Bet on Beliefs?”

   Unlocks: a stronger **Substack essay / intent-engineering bridge**: “Intent Engineering Needs Prediction Markets, But Not Market Sovereignty.” This connects Sean’s I-5 framework to Polymarket without collapsing intent into price signals. Current failure mode: generic “agents + markets” futurism. New artifact: a governance argument with a named opposing framework.

3. **Add “token cash-flow accrual audit,” anchored on Chris Burniske’s _Cryptoassets_**

   Missing piece: “crypto cash flow” is too loose. Add a hard distinction between **protocol revenue**, **token-holder accrual**, **staking yield**, **subsidized emissions**, and **real external cash flow**. Pattern: `Who pays? In what asset? Who receives? By what enforceable mechanism? Is yield revenue, inflation, or risk transfer?`

   Exemplifies it: Chris Burniske and Jack Tatar, _Cryptoassets: The Innovative Investor’s Guide to Bitcoin and Beyond_.

   Unlocks: a portfolio-facing **crypto cash-flow diligence template** or agentic evaluator that can reject fake-yield opportunities before they enter the research queue. Current failure mode: “interesting market opportunity.” New artifact: “underwriting memo with accrual mechanics and fraud/yield-risk flags.”
