# TMAY — Per-Company Variations

> **Rule:** the TMAY's section 4 (`Why Here`) is the only part that changes per company — and it **must** change. Delivering the generic `[SPECIFIC PRODUCT SURFACE]` placeholder signals you didn't do the homework. Fill a row before each interview; a filled row is a precondition for taking the loop.
>
> Each row needs three things: the **product surface** you name (specific, current, researched), the **archetype word** that matches the role (agent-platform / context-engineering / vendor-eval / forward-deployed / agent-quality), and a **one-line bridge** from your arc to their work.

## The contained layoff answer

> Separate from TMAY but adjacent — this is the **swapped Q2** from the Task 19 mock plan: *"Walk me through what happened with The Block and how it reset your search."* (Swapped from "Why are you leaving The Block?" because you were laid off, not departing.) Interviewers will ask some version of this. Have the contained version ready so you never improvise it.

**The answer (target ~45–60s):**

> **"The Block went through a cost-cutting layoff in early May and my role was eliminated — it wasn't performance-related, and my former president is one of my references. / What it reset was my focus. Before, I was splitting energy across a PM role and my own AI building on the side. / After, I pointed all of it at one target: AI PM roles, with proof. / In eight weeks I've shipped an MCP server on the public registry, an eval suite, a control architecture, and a live agent-observability dashboard. / So the honest version is — the layoff is the reason I stopped evangelizing AI and started shipping it."**

**Why it's built this way (the rubric dims it exercises hardest):**

- **Information control:** one factual sentence on the layoff (cost-cutting, not performance, reference intact) — then you *stop*. You never volunteer org drama, names, or grievances. The "my president is a reference" clause closes the door on the performance question without you having to deny anything.
- **Weakness-flipping:** the layoff becomes the *inflection that produced the artifacts*, not a wound. The last line is the flip, said as a clean close.
- **Impact specificity:** you name the four shipped things — registry, eval suite, control architecture, dashboard. Specificity is what makes "I used the time well" believable instead of a platitude.

**Do-not-do list for this answer:**
- Don't explain *why* the company cut costs (the turnover, the new CEO) — out of scope, scores you down on information control.
- Don't sound rehearsed-bitter. Calm, factual, zen — your own communication baseline.
- Don't run past 60s. This is a contained answer, not a second TMAY.
