# Resume Funnel-Role Decision

> Phase 1 Task 1.1 of the [resume-council plan](2026-05-17-resume-council-plan.md). Locks the constraints for the V2 redraft + the council prompt before either is touched.

## The one-sentence role (the answer)

> **The 2026-05-19 resume is a *secondary* artifact whose job is to showcase my previous professional work with the ATS keywords and metrics proving what I did, how I did it, and what the results were — without compromising my personal projects that show that I'm more than my previous professional background. I build on the side for fun and because it's a passion of mine that I can bring to your company.**

## The package this resume lives inside

The resume is not a standalone artifact. It's one of four pieces of a single pristine package:

1. **AI PM Portfolio** — `seanwinslow.com/transactions` (going live Mon 2026-05-19 per unified roadmap Task 1 Step 3)
2. **Proof of building in public** — Substack post 1 ships Fri 2026-05-22 (Sedaris voice); LinkedIn syndication Wed cadence
3. **GitHub repo presence** — Code-Brain (118 skills / 13 subagents / 14 hooks / 17 SDK agents, Karpathy framing in README L3) + `sw-mcp-intent-engineering` (live on npm + MCP registry as of 2026-05-12)
4. **Resume itself** — showcase of professional work + personal passion projects + skills

**The package coherence is the differentiator.** The other three artifacts prove the resume isn't lying. Without all four, no single piece carries enough weight to clear the 2026 AI PM bar.

## Answers to the three questions

### Q1: ATS-mediated apps (LinkedIn Easy Apply, Greenhouse/Lever portals)

**The resume is an attached requirement — but a very important one.** Resume isn't the lead artifact; the package is. But for ATS-mediated funnels (the bulk of Tier-1 PM listings), the resume PDF is the file that gets parsed, scored, and used to gate the phone screen. So "secondary in the package" ≠ "low quality."

### Q2: Warm-intro / direct-recruiter conversations

**Both go.** It's customary to showcase the work AND send the resume. **The resume must have the `seanwinslow.com/transactions` URL on it** — that's how the package coheres.

### Q3: ATS keyword scoring vs. human-reader signal density

**A mixture of both, not pure either side.** Many companies hiring this cycle won't have a warm intro available — the ATS funnel is the path. So keyword presence matters. **But not keyword vomit.** Recruiters and hiring managers can smell stuffing from a mile away. The line: include the keywords where they're defensible and earned by actual evidence on the bullet, and cut them where they'd read as performative.

## Implications for the redraft + council prompt

Translation of the above into redraft constraints (these flow into Phase 5 council-triage decision rules):

- **The resume header MUST link `seanwinslow.com/transactions`.** Hard prereq: the site must be live before any resume goes out. (Personal-site deploy target: Mon 2026-05-19 — same day applications start. The plan's link-health gate at Task 6.3 enforces this.)
- **The resume must surface passion-project evidence**, not just Block PM credential signal. The Selected Projects section earns its keep — it's the load-bearing section for "more than my previous professional background."
- **The resume keeps Block-era metrics + result-orientation** ("what I did, how I did it, what the results were") — even where the CIIA-scrub neuters specifics, the verb + capability + measured-outcome shape is preserved.
- **JD-keyword inclusion is permission-based, not mandate-based.** Council recommendations to "add 'agentic' + 'evals' + 'MCP'" only get APPLY-bucket treatment if the bullet they're added to has the evidence to back them up. Keyword vomit goes to REJECT.
- **AI-PM-as-master question (Council Q1) leans toward "no, keep master agnostic."** The package's coherence is the differentiator across all three archetypes (AI PM / Tech PM / Creative PM); leading with AI PM on the master would weaken the "more than my previous professional background" claim that Sean wants preserved. Reverse only if the council shows unanimous strong evidence that recruiter-rate optimization wins.
