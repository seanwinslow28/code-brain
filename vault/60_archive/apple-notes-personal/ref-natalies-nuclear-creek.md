# Natalie's Nuclear Creek

Natalie's Nuclear Creek

Born an only child in the suburbs of Staten Island, NY, and raised by two homebodies, Sean spent a decent part of his childhood… alone. A decade before the social media boom, he had to look inward and figure out some creative ways to pass the time. Some of it was spent in front of the television binging on multiple shows and cartoons. The rest was spent with a notepad and a pencil, drawing and creating his own stories and characters. As he got older, he started making homemade movies with cousins and friends. Most of these consisted of a simple idea and lots of improvisation. Always trying to top the other's jokes in an attempt to make the other person break character. This sparked his interest in comedy and film.

Fast forward to 2020 where he found himself with way too much time on his hands. Aside from drawing and binging on animation, he also took online Animation classes at The School Of Visual Arts and Youtube University. After spending numerous hours on backgrounds, character designs, voice work, and editing, Sean created his first animated short called "Break Time". 



Whether it was diving into my own work of writing and illustrating, listening to numerous albums or viewing billions of hours of television, the arts had a significant role in my sanity throughout the pandemic. 

I would like to convey the importance of art and fellow artists during low points in humanity by showing what would happen if they were stripped away and why they should be kept in our lives… while also throwing in a fart joke or two. 

One of my favorite types of humor is what I call "Casual Chaos" (Ex: "Ah, I just shot Marvin in the face" - Pulp Fiction). Characters in chaotic situations, but it's also the norm, so they just carry on with their business as if they see this every day. What better way to portray this type of humor than with mutants, ravagers, and cannibals in a high school setting? 

14 years after a nuclear apocalypse, An artistic and passionate teen butts heads with an oppressive high school principal after he terminates the schools Arts Programs.
