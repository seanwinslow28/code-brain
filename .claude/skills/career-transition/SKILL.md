# Career Transition: PM → Animation PM/Producer

## Purpose

Guide the transition from tech Product Management to Animation Production Management or Producing. These roles share more DNA than either industry admits — scope management, stakeholder communication, resource allocation, timeline negotiation, and shipping under constraints. This skill bridges the terminology, builds the portfolio narrative, and maps the networking path.

## When to Use

- Planning the transition from PM to animation production
- Building a portfolio that translates PM experience to production skills
- Preparing for animation industry interviews
- Networking at animation festivals (Annecy, Ottawa, GLAS, etc.)
- Understanding production management roles and titles
- Bridging tech PM vocabulary to animation production vocabulary

| Backlog prioritization | Shot prioritization | "I triaged 200+ items by impact and dependencies, cutting scope to hit deadline" |

**Example 3: Portfolio Strategy**
```
User: "What should my portfolio look like for production roles?"
Claude: [Uses career-transition]

## Production Portfolio Structure

Animation production roles care about PROOF YOU CAN SHIP,
not your animation reel. Your portfolio is different from
an artist's or animator's portfolio.

### Page 1: Production Case Study (your animated short)
- Project overview: what, timeline, team size, budget
- Your role: production management decisions you made
- Timeline visual: Gantt or milestone chart showing planned vs actual
- Challenge/resolution: a real problem you solved (scope cut, schedule
  slip, team conflict) and the outcome
- Deliverable: link to the finished film or festival screening

### Page 2: PM-as-Production Case Studies (2-3 from tech)
Reframe your best PM work in production language:
- "Shipped a v1 product in 16 weeks with 8 engineers"
  → "Managed a 16-week production with an 8-person team"
- Show: timeline, scope decisions, stakeholder management, delivery

### Page 3: Skills & Tools
- Production tools: Shotgun/ftrack/Flow (learn one), Google Sheets
  (production breakdowns), Frame.io (review)
- PM tools you already know: JIRA, Confluence, Linear, Figma
- Soft skills: vendor management, budget tracking, scheduling

### Page 4: About + Contact
- Your narrative (see "Transition Narrative" below)
- Links: LinkedIn, email, portfolio site
```

## The Terminology Bridge

The single most important thing for the transition: **speak their language**.

| Tech PM Term | Animation Production Term | Notes |
|:-------------|:------------------------|:------|
| Sprint | Production week / schedule block | Animation doesn't use "sprints" but the concept is identical |
| Epic | Sequence | A group of related shots that tell a story beat |
| User Story | Shot | The atomic unit of production work |
| Story Points | Footage (feet + frames) | Effort measured in output quantity |
| Backlog | Shot list / task list | Unsorted remaining work |
| Velocity | Footage per week | Team output rate |
| Sprint Review | Dailies / Weeklies | Regular review of in-progress work |
| Retrospective | Post-mortem / wrap report | What went well, what didn't |
| Product Owner | Director / Showrunner | The creative decision-maker |
| Stakeholder | Executive Producer / Client | The person who controls the budget |
| Roadmap | Production schedule / Gantt | The timeline document |
| Ship date | Delivery date / Lock date | When the final deliverable is due |
| MVP | First pass / rough cut | The minimum viable version |
| Technical debt | Retakes / fixes | Work that needs to be redone |
| A/B test | Test screening | Showing work to an audience for feedback |
| KPIs | Delivery metrics | On-time %, budget variance, retake rate |

## Role Map

### Animation Production Titles (smallest → largest scope)

| Title | Scope | Equivalent PM Level |
|:------|:------|:-------------------|
| Production Coordinator | Manages logistics for a department | Associate PM |
| Production Manager | Manages schedule/budget for a show or section | PM |
| Line Producer | Manages entire production budget and timeline | Senior PM |
| Producer | Creative + business oversight of a project | Director of Product |
| Executive Producer | Portfolio of projects, studio-level decisions | VP Product |
| Showrunner (TV) | Creative authority + production oversight | CPO (rare hybrid) |

### Where to Enter

For a PM with 5+ years of tech experience, realistic entry points:
1. **Production Manager** at a small-to-mid studio (10-50 person productions)
2. **Production Coordinator** at a major studio (if willing to step down in title for industry cred)
3. **Producer on your own short** (self-funded or grant-funded — the strongest portfolio piece)

## Production Management Tools

### What Studios Use

| Tool | Purpose | Learn Priority |
|:-----|:--------|:--------------|
| Shotgun (ShotGrid) | Industry standard production tracking | High — learn this |
| ftrack | Production tracking (mid-size studios) | Medium |
| Flow (formerly Shotgun) | Autodesk's rebrand of ShotGrid | Same as Shotgun |
| Frame.io | Review and approval (video/animation) | High — easy to learn |
| Google Sheets | Production breakdowns, budgets | You already know this |
| Deadline / Tractor | Render farm management | Low (technical, not PM) |

### What You Already Know That Transfers

| PM Tool | Animation Use |
|:--------|:-------------|
| JIRA / Linear | Some studios use these (especially VFX/games crossover) |
| Confluence / Notion | Internal wikis, production bibles |
| Figma | Storyboard/design review (some studios) |
| Slack | Studio communication (universally used) |
| Google Workspace | Budget tracking, scheduling, shared docs |

## Transition Narrative Template

Use this to frame your career story in applications, interviews, and networking:

```markdown
## My Transition Story (2-3 minute pitch)

**The Bridge:**
"I've spent [X] years as a Product Manager shipping [products/features]
with teams of [size] across [complexity]. Animation production and
product management are the same discipline with different vocabulary —
scope management, stakeholder communication, resource allocation, and
shipping under constraints."

**The Proof:**
"To prove that to myself and to studios, I produced my own animated
short: [Title]. I managed [team size], [budget], and [timeline], handling
everything from storyboard scheduling through final delivery. It screened
at [festival] / is currently in festival submission."

**The Why:**
"I've been passionate about animation since [personal connection].
I want to bring the rigor of tech product management — data-informed
decisions, clear communication, systematic process — to animation
production, where those skills are in high demand but short supply."

**The Ask:**
"I'm looking for [Production Manager / Line Producer] roles at studios
working on [type of content]. I bring [specific PM skill] and [specific
production experience] and I'm ready to [specific next step]."
```

## 90-Day Transition Plan

| Week | Focus | Actions |
|:-----|:------|:--------|
| 1-2 | Learn the language | Study this skill's terminology bridge. Watch production-focused talks (not animation tutorials). |
| 3-4 | Learn the tools | Take a ShotGrid/ftrack tutorial. Set up a production tracking sheet for your short. |
| 5-8 | Build the proof | Start your animated short (even a 30-second piece). Document the production, not just the art. |
| 9-10 | Build the network | Identify 20 production professionals on LinkedIn. Engage with their posts. Send 5 cold messages. |
| 11-12 | Apply and pitch | Update portfolio with production case studies. Apply to 3-5 roles. Submit to 1-2 festivals. |
