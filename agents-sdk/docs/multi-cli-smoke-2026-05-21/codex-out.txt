1. **Add “Forensic Moral Essayist” Mode**

Anchor it on **Joan Didion, “The White Album”**.

Sentence pattern: **reported fact + withheld judgment + destabilizing qualification + moral aftershock**.

Example pattern: “We tell ourselves stories in order to live” is the famous compressed version, but the useful machinery is Didion’s habit of putting evidence on the table, then letting the implied indictment arrive late.

What it adds: a mode for **diagnostic essays about institutions, taste, technology culture, and personal complicity**. The current five modes can observe, intensify, riff, deadpan, or hybridize, but they do not give Sean a cold instrument for saying: *this is what happened, this is what everyone pretended it meant, and here is the rot beneath the explanation*. It would unlock essays where Sean needs authority without performance.

2. **Add “Architectural Systems Narrator” Mode**

Anchor it on **John McPhee, “The Search for Marvin Gardens”**.

Sentence pattern: **concrete scene + structural analogy + hidden system + return to the scene with altered meaning**.

McPhee’s Monopoly-board/New Jersey structure is the model: he does not merely describe place; he builds an essay whose form reveals the argument. The technique is not “clear exposition.” It is **structural embodiment**.

What it adds: a mode for **technical strategy essays, company operating memos, architecture notes, and product retrospectives** where the argument needs to be felt as a system, not stated as a thesis. Sean’s current modes are voice-forward. This would add **form-forward writing**: pieces whose organization carries the insight. It would keep technical writing from becoming either Gonzo chaos or professional restraint.

3. **Add “Intimate Intellectual Pressure” Mode**

Anchor it on **Maggie Nelson, “Bluets”**.

Sentence pattern: **numbered fragment + private confession + citation/idea shard + unresolved turn**.

The useful move is Nelson’s pressure between aphorism, research note, erotic confession, and philosophical doubt. Each fragment is small, but the accumulation becomes emotional argument.

What it adds: a mode for **personal knowledge-vault essays, grief/obsession notes, creative research logs, and hybrid memoir-thinking pieces**. The current five modes do not fully support vulnerability without either joking, stylizing, or accelerating past it. This mode would let Sean write intellectually serious personal material that stays exposed, precise, and unfinished in the right way. It gives him a format for saying: *I do not have a conclusion, but I have arranged the evidence of my attention.*
