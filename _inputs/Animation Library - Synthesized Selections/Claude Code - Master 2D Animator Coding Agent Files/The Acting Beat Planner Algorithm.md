Here is the **Acting Beat Planner Algorithm**, a structured logic engine for generating character performance data. This system translates abstract narrative inputs into concrete timeline events and pose constraints, ensuring every frame serves a distinct narrative purpose. \#\#\# **1\. Mental Model** \* **Concept:** "Acting is Doing." Animation is not just moving; it is a sequence of **Beats** (thoughts/emotions) strung together like beads on a necklace. A beat occurs when a character’s thought process changes due to a stimulus or obstacle. \* **Core Rule:** A character plays an action until something happens to make them play a different action. \* **Citation:** "Think of beads in a necklace... You can’t play an action for a while and then just hang out... Acting is reacting." 1, 2\. \--- \#\#\# **2\. The Beat Generation Loop** The algorithm processes the inputs through a four-stage state machine to produce a single **Acting Beat**. \#\#\#\# **Phase 1: The Thought (Reaction/Processing)** \* **Logic:** The character perceives the stimulus *before* moving the body. \* **Sequence:** 1\. **Eye Lead:** Eyes move to the target 2–3 frames before the head turns. 9, 10 2\. **Processing Pause:** Hold the pose while the brain "crunches" the data. \* **Frame Budget:** 6–12 frames (approx. 0.25s – 0.5s) for the thought to register. 11, 12 \* **Pose Constraint:** **Fixed Head.** If the character is thinking, the body may freeze (a "Moving Hold"), but the eyes remain active. 13, 14 \#\#\#\# **Phase 2: The Anticipation (Telegraphing)** \* **Logic:** The character prepares to act. "Broadcast Intent." \* **Rule:** Move in the **opposite direction** of the intended action first. \* **Vector Math:** Anticipation\_Vector \= \-1 \* Action\_Vector \* Intensity. 15, 16 \* **Frame Budget:** \* **Standard:** 4–8 frames. 17 \* **Surprise/Shock:** 1–2 frames (invisible anticipation). 18, 17 \#\#\#\# **Phase 3: The Action (Change)** \* **Logic:** The execution of the objective. The release of energy. \* **Spacing Rule:** \* **Aggressive/Fast:** Use "Ones" (24fps) or **Smears** (1 frame duration) if velocity \> width. 19, 20 \* **Normal/Heavy:** Use "Twos" (12fps) with **Cubic Easing** (Slow-In/Slow-Out). 19, 21 \* **Arc Constraint:** The path of action must follow a Bézier curve (Arc), never a straight line. 22, 23 \#\#\#\# **Phase 4: The Settle (Result/Feedback)** \* **Logic:** The character assesses the result of their action. Did they succeed? \* **Pose Rule:** **The Golden Pose.** Establish a clear, readable silhouette that defines the new emotional state. \* **Overshoot:** The body moves past the final pose and settles back (e.g., Target\_Pos \+ 10% \-\> Target\_Pos). 24, 25 \* **Frame Budget:** Minimum 6–12 frames. Any hold shorter than 3 frames is invisible. 26, 11 \--- \#\#\# **4\.
